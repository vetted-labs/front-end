1. Executive summary

Verdict — overall 78/100. The real-flow infrastructure is genuinely good: a working headless EIP-1193 wallet shim, deterministic anvil snapshot/revert, end-to-end-typed helpers, and product-language test.step() labels. The mocked lane was recently triaged (13 render-smokes deleted, 29 re-segmented). The suite's weak points are coverage holes, not infrastructure: candidate signup, messaging, notifications, governance, and the company hiring pipeline have no real-flow coverage at all, and 8 vetting scenarios are quarantined behind backend divergence DIV-010 (VettingManager.createVettingSession is onlyOwner, BE signer ≠ owner). Endorsement coverage is the strongest end-to-end story — 8/8 outcome states wired to chain + BE invariants.

What's strong

  • Wallet shim foundation — 28 smoke tests all green, provably drives RainbowKit→wagmi→on-chain.
  • Fixture composition (fixtures.ts) — worker-scoped manifest + test-scoped cleanState snapshot/revert is textbook.
  • Endorsement suite — 8 scenarios cover the full bid→hire→reward/refund/dispute/retention lifecycle with on-chain assertions.
  • Oracle layer — 27 pure tests independently implement protocol math (consensus, slashing, rewards) per the CEO consensus spec.
  • Typed helpers — real-flow contract handles, viem types, and finalization payloads propagate without any.

What's weak / missing

  • 8 vetting scenarios quarantined (DIV-010) — the entire core review-chain at-rest until BE/contract owner aligns.
  • No candidate signup / login real-flow. No messaging or notifications interaction tests anywhere.
  • Governance flow — mocked only; zero on-chain coverage.
  • Withdrawal finalization (Phase 5) — pending, blocked by DIV-008/009.
  • Rubric-score scraping in ui-review.ts has 4 fallback patterns — symptom of missing data-testid.
  • guild-mocks.ts — 38 KB / 1378 LOC monolith of nested fixture objects; difficult to maintain.
